Selling Crochet Items at Markets

Becoming a Vendor

When choosing to sell what you make, there are many things that need to be considered. For example, where do you want to sell, who do you want to sell to, what do you want to sell, how far are you willing to drive to sell these items? All of these have to be considered together because it determines how you are going to spend your time.

To start off, you will want to spend some time going to a variety of craft fairs in the vacinity that you are willing to drive to in order to see how well the foot traffic (amount of people) is. This part is definitely important because you do not want to show up somewhere that you already know does not have a good turn out with people showing up. Additionally, you should be taking notes about how other crochet vendors have their pricing set and how they have their booth set up. Presentation is key to some customers. Some like an open and inviting booth, while others like one where they do not have to walk inside the booth in order to see what you have. It is hard to find a happy medium sometimes and you just have to go with what you think will work well with your inventory.

The next thing you want to look at is how much it is to rent a booth at each spot. Most places will either have you pay a fee upfront, dontate an item, or have you pay them a percentage of your sales. If you are just starting out, I would not recommend doing ones where the place takes a percentage of your sales because that can seem a little demeaning. Additionally, always make sure you are checking with the actual event coordinator and not some random person on facebook saying they are "helping find people for a craft fair". This is the most common scam people are faceing currently in the crochet community.

Common Market Terms

Inventory
Products a vendor has made and brought with to sell
Vendor
A person/business that is selling at a market
Booth
A space a vendor rents to display and sell their products

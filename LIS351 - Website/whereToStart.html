Where to Start Crocheting

Skill Level

Beginner

For people starting out, I would recommend starting with the Susan Bates set of hooks and Red Heart yarn. These will be the easiest to work with for people getting started because the yarn is sturdy enough that it will not unravel or slide off your hook. To start off, I would recommend looking at HappyBerryCrochet for help as she is who I learned from when first starting amigurumi at least. She has lots of free tutorials posted on YouTube and even goes slow enough to make sure people of all levels can understand her instructions.

Intermediate

For people who have been crocheting for a bit already and are ready for the next step, I would start looking at more in depth and bigger patterns. Thicker yarns could be used, or thinner ones. Learning many different stitches would be helpful. Additionally, longer patterns could be looked at, or even the diagram patterns could be attempted. At this point in crocheting looking at different brands of yarn and branching out to see what you would like to use, based on weight and softness.

Advanced

For people who have crocheted a long time, the new trendy chaos blanket would be a great project. However, at this point, most stitches should be learned, being able to replicate complex projects multiple times (if you are planning on selling), and being comfortable with color changes in the middle of rows should be natural. Tension should be the same for the whole project. Being able to read a pattern and modify it to your needs is an amazing skill to have at this level.

Supplies needed

  • Crochet hooks
  • Yarn
  • Scissors
  • Stitch markers
  • Yarn needles
